About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at MILA and Concordia University, my research focuses on developing efficient learning algorithms, with a particular emphasis on distributed and federated training. My work has been presented at several prestigious machine learning conferences, including NeurIPS. I am supported by numerous awards and scholarships, including the highly regarded FRQNT Doctoral Training Scholarship, valued at $91,667 over four years, and a 2024 RBC Borealis Fellowship, awarded annually to ten exceptional Ph.D. students in the field of machine learning. These awards provide invaluable support for my research. In addition to my doctoral studies, I interned at Sony, onsite in Tokyo, Japan for three months, where I contributed to research on disentangled edits of 2D images by exploring the properties of latent spaces in diffusion models.
